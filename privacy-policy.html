2. What data we collect and why

2.1 Account and profile data

When you set up Yoley you provide:

  • First name and last name
  • Email address and phone number
  • Business / trading name and trade type
  • Business address and postcode
  • VAT registration number (optional)
  • Companies House number (optional)

Why: To personalise the app, pre-fill documents you send to customers, and identify your business on quotes and invoices.

2.2 Business records you create

The app stores records you create, including:

  • Customer contact details (name, address, email, phone)
  • Quotes, invoices, expenses, purchase orders, work orders and job diary entries
  • Bank transaction data if you use Open Banking (Pro feature)
  • Recurring payment schedules
  • Subcontractor details

Why: To provide the core functionality of the app — you cannot use Yoley without creating these records.

2.3 Payment data

When you take a card payment or generate a Pay by Link via Monek, we pass to Monek:

  • Invoice reference and amount
  • Your Monek Merchant ID
  • Your customer's email address

We do not store card numbers or CVV codes. Those are handled entirely by Monek's PCI-DSS certified systems.

2.4 Subscription data

If you subscribe to Yoley Pro, Apple manages the billing and subscription status via the App Store. We receive only a transaction entitlement token — we never see your payment card details.

2.5 Technical and usage data

We collect minimal technical data to keep the app working:

  • App version and iOS version (for crash diagnostics via Apple's standard reporting)
  • Sync timestamps via iCloud / CloudKit

We do not use third-party analytics SDKs (e.g. Firebase, Mixpanel). We do not track your activity across other apps or websites.

4. Where your data is stored

All business records you create in Yoley are stored:

  • On your device — using Apple's SwiftData framework (local SQLite database)
  • In your iCloud account — synced via Apple CloudKit, so only you (and Apple, per their own Privacy Policy) can access them

We do not operate our own cloud database for your business records. We cannot read your invoices, customers, or expenses — they live in your iCloud account, not ours.

7. Your rights under UK GDPR

As a UK resident you have the following rights. To exercise any of them, contact us at privacy@yoley.com. We will respond within 30 days.

  • Right of access — obtain a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention obligations)
  • Right to restriction — ask us to pause processing while a dispute is resolved
  • Right to data portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right not to be subject to automated decisions — we do not make solely automated decisions that significantly affect you

If you are unhappy with how we handle your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

  • Website: ico.org.uk
  • Helpline: 0303 123 1113

8. Cookies and tracking

The Yoley app does not use cookies. It does not contain advertising SDKs or cross-app tracking. Apple's App Tracking Transparency framework is respected; we make no ATT requests because we do not track you across third-party apps or websites.

9. Children's data

Yoley is a business tool intended for users aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 18. If you believe a minor has provided us with personal data, please contact privacy@yoley.com and we will delete it promptly.

10. Security

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including:

  • Monek API credentials stored in the iOS Keychain (hardware-backed encryption)
  • All data synced via Apple CloudKit is encrypted in transit and at rest
  • Optional Face ID / Touch ID app lock to prevent unauthorised device access
