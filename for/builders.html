Building is cashflow. A £60k kitchen extension has to be billed in the right stages or you’re funding a client’s kitchen out of your pocket for six weeks. Yoley is built around payment schedules — split a quote into a deposit, a first-fix stage, a second-fix stage and a final — and raise the invoice for each milestone without re-typing anything.

If you’re a main contractor hiring subcontractors, CIS deductions are calculated on the invoice automatically. 20% for verified, 30% for unverified, 0% for gross-status — free, on the Free plan. Compare that to QuickBooks where you need the £32/month Plus tier just to unlock CIS.

Whether you do extensions, loft conversions, new builds, groundworks, brickwork, conservatories or landlord maintenance, Yoley is the fastest way to keep the paperwork off the kitchen table and the cash moving.

🛠 A typical day

Site survey → stage invoices → paid

1

Week 1 — Survey and price the extension

You measure up a single-storey rear extension. Back at home you build the quote with 24 line items, then split it into a 5% deposit, 40% first-fix, 40% second-fix, 15% completion using Yoley’s payment schedule. Send. Client accepts the same day.

2

Week 3 — First-fix complete

Tap the quote, tap Raise next stage invoice. 40% first-fix invoice goes straight to the client with a payment link. Paid by card that afternoon — 1.49%, in your bank Monday.

3

Week 4 — Pay the plasterer

Your plasterer sends you a £2,400 invoice. You raise a CIS invoice back to him from the app. Yoley calculates the 20% deduction on labour, leaves materials untouched. He signs, you pay the net — and the deduction is logged for your monthly CIS return.

4

Week 8 — Completion

Job finished, client walks through with a snag list. You raise the final 15% invoice from your phone on site. She pays by Tap to Pay straight onto your iPhone. Done.
