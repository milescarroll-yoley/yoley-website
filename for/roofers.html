Roofing is the most weather-exposed trade on the Yoley roster. A three-day re-roof booked into a week of April showers becomes a five-day job with a wet loft and a nervous client. Yoley’s Pro diary pulls a live 5-day forecast for every job address so you can spot the problem before it’s on your van.

For pricing, Yoley’s quotes let you attach drone photos, close-up shots of missing tiles, and reference product images of slates or concrete tiles — so the customer can see exactly what they’re paying for. Take a deposit the moment they accept so the scaffolders, tiles and membrane are ordered against their money, not yours.

Emergency work, felt repairs, fascia and soffit replacements, full re-roofs, flat roof installs, lead work or Velux fits — Yoley keeps the admin side on your phone so your weekends are yours again. If you work as a CIS subcontractor on new builds, Yoley handles the deductions automatically, free.

🛠 A typical re-roof

Survey → weather-aware booking → paid

1

Day 1 — Survey and photos

You go up the ladder, photograph the failed felt, note the tile count, check the flashings. In the van you build the quote with photos attached to each line item: strip + dispose, new membrane, re-tile, new ridge tiles, lead valley, scaffold. 30% deposit schedule.

2

Day 3 — Accepted, deposit paid

Client accepts the quote online and pays the 30% deposit by card at 1.49%. Your 5-day forecast for their address shows dry Mon–Wed, wet Thu. You book the job Mon–Wed.

3

Week 2 — Strip, membrane, re-tile

Scaffolders up Monday morning. You strip, felt, batten and tile across the three-day weather window. Each trip to SIG gets the receipt scanned. Wednesday evening the roof is weather-tight.

4

Thursday — Final invoice, paid

Client walks the site, happy. You raise the 70% balance invoice from your phone. She taps her card to your iPhone. In your account Monday — and your weekend is still yours.
