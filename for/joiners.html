Bespoke joinery is a trade where the wrong quote costs you thousands. Oak prices move, workshop hours stack up, and clients need to see the detail before they commit. Yoley gives you line-by-line quotes with photos attached, a deposit invoice you can raise the moment a client says yes, and an expenses log that remembers which receipt was for which job.

Whether you’re fitting a staircase, hanging doors across a new build, building a fitted wardrobe, doing kitchen carcass work, or handling full first-and-second-fix for a main contractor, Yoley keeps the admin on your phone so your evenings aren’t buried in paper. And if you’re on site as a CIS subcontractor, CIS is handled automatically, free.

Pair it with Tap to Pay on iPhone at 0.99% and you can take a card payment the moment a set of made-to-measure wardrobes gets signed off — no payment link, no chasing, no waiting for a BACS transfer.

Five reasons it fits bespoke work

📷

Quotes with reference photos

Attach site photos, sketches and mood-board images to the line items on a quote. Clients see exactly what they’re getting before they sign.

💵

Deposits before you order timber

Split the quote into a 30% deposit and 70% on completion. Raise the deposit invoice the minute the quote is accepted — protects you when the oak price jumps.

🏡

Workshop and site receipts, sorted

Snap every Howdens, MKM, Travis Perkins, IronmongeryDirect and Screwfix receipt. Tag against the job. Material cost on every project is already totalled.

🏦

CIS on new-build work

Doing second-fix for a main contractor? Enter the contractor’s CIS details once, and every invoice you send them has the 20% deduction already calculated.

💳

Tap to Pay on handover

Bespoke wardrobes fitted, client standing in the room, happy. Tap their card to your iPhone and take the balance at 0.99%. Job done, paid, go home.

🛠 A typical project

Consultation → workshop → fit → paid

1

Day 1 — Consultation in the client’s home

You measure the alcove, photograph the space, sketch a couple of options. On the way home you build the quote on your iPhone: line items for materials, finishings, machining and fit. Attach the photos. Add a 30% deposit schedule. Send.

2

Day 3 — Deposit paid, timber ordered

Client accepts and pays the 30% deposit by card at 1.49%. You order the oak from your local timber merchant, snap the purchase receipt, tag it to the job.

3

Week 2 — Workshop

Machine, sand, finish. Each trip to Howdens or IronmongeryDirect gets its receipt snapped. At end-of-week your project cost is visible in the expenses tab — you can see the margin already.

4

Week 3 — Fit and final invoice

On site, pieces fit, client signs off. You raise the 70% balance invoice on your phone. They tap their card to your iPhone with Tap to Pay. In your bank Monday.
